Lesson 1

Adjectives

Adjectives

  • It is used to describe words

Kinds of Adjectives

  • Descriptive Adjectives
    • Used to describe the quality of nouns and pronouns.
      • Examples:
      • Size
      • Shape
      • Nationality
  • Demonstrative Adjectives
  • Used to point the distance of the noun and the speaker.
    • Examples:
    • Singular
      • This is an apple. (near)
      • That is an apple. (far)
    • Plural
      • These are apples. (near)
      • Those are apples. (far)

Conjuction

Conjuction

  • It is defined as a part of the speech that joints two words, phrases, or clauses together.

  • It is to like other words, phrases, or clauses together.

    • Examples:
    • I like writing. I like reading. I don't like speaking.
    • I like writng and reading, but I don't like speaking.

3 Types of Conjuction

  1. Coordinating

    • Connects words, phrases, or clauses that have the same value in a sentence.

    • Connects a noun with another noun or a prepositional phrases with another prepositional phrases.

    • 7 Coordinating

      1. For (Since/Because)
        • Shows relationship between things.
        • Must pust comma (,) before the conjuctions when combining sentence.
          • Examples:
          • I drink coffee for, I am sleepy.
          • We listen to the band, for we idolize them.
      2. And (In addition to)
        • Connecting things that are alike.
          • Examples:
          • She likes to sing and dance.
          • I bought dresses, and shoes for the prom.
      3. Nor (Not)
        • States a negative choice.
          • Examples:
          • He don't want to sing nor dance in front of the people.
          • I couldn't deliver the message, nor could she.
      4. But (However)
        • Connect things that are different.
          • Examples:
          • I want to eat pizza but not burger.
          • All of them want to eat burger, but no one wanted to buy it.
      5. Or (Either)
        • States a choice.
          • Examples:
          • Do you want hot coffee or iced coffee?
          • We could just read the story, or we just watch the movie.
      6. Yet (But)
        • Shows a change.
          • Examples:
          • The ramen was cold and sogggy, yet we still bought it.
          • He is a strict leader, yet many students admire him.
      7. So (Therefore)
        • Shows a relationship between.
          • Examples:
          • I'm craving for a pizza, so I ordered 2 boxes for dinner.
          • All of them want to eat pizza, so the adviser treats them in the cafeteria.
  2. Correlative
    • The "tag-team". Work in pairs.
    • Either... or
      • Choose between the two choices means " this or that", "one or the other".
        • Examples:
        • Either you eat all the cake or share it to your friends.
        • They either recite now or after the discussion.
    • Neither... nor
      • None of the choices.
        • Examples:
        • My cat like neither milk nor juice.
        • Neither the professor nor the students was at school.
    • Both... and
      • Refers to the two things together.
      • The verb takes plural form.
        • Examples:
        • Both milktea and coffee are not good for you.
        • I can play both guitar and violin.
    • Not only... but also
      • Connects two similar ideas.
        • Examples:
        • The movie is not only funny, but also thrilling.
        • NCT is not only visuals but also vocals.
  3. Subordinating
    • Links a dependent clause to an independent clause.
      • Dependent Clause
        • Incomplete thought
      • Independent Clause
        • Complete thoughts.
    • When
      • Time related conjuction.
        • Example:
        • I met NCT 127 ( IND ) when it was one day before my birthday ( DEP ).
    • Until
      • Means up to a particular time.
        • Example:
        • The sale is until July 7th.
    • Unless
      • Means 'except when', 'if not'.
        • Example:
        • You don't need to finish it unless you want to.
    • However
      • Contrast
        • Example:
        • I like squash; however , I cannot eat brocolli.
    • Since
      • Used to show result
        • Example:
        • I made this bracelet since you liked it so much.

Lesson 3

Modal Verbs

Modal

  • A modal is an auxiliary or helping verb that is used to express ability, capability, possibility, request, permisssion, or obligation

  • Can
    • Be able to.
      • Example:
      • Ability
        • I can ride a board.
      • Permission
        • Can I ride the bike?
      • Request
        • Can you lend me your money?
  • Could
    • Used to indicate possibility.
      • Examples:
      • Past Ability
        • He could swim when he was 5.
      • Request
        • Could you say it again more slowly?
      • Suggestion
        • We could read it or just watch the movie.
  • May
    • Expressing permission.
      • Examples:
      • Asking Permission
        • May I borrow your notes?
      • Giving Permisson
        • Yes, you may borrow it.
  • Might
    • Past of May .
      • Example:
      • Possibility
        • Your folder might be in the small cabinet.
  • Shall
    • Expressing a strong assertion or intention.
      • Example:
      • Obligation
        • The violator shall pay fine of $ 500.
  • Suggestion
    • Shall we call them?
  • Should
    • Used to indicate what is probable.
      • Examples:
      • Suggestion
        • You should eat more
      • Advice
        • We should focus more on our shop.
  • Will
    • Desire, wish.
      • Examples:
      • Request
        • Will you please help me with my studies?
      • Promise
        • I will call you at noon.
  • Would
    • Past of Will
      Examples:
      • Offer
        • Would like some coffee?
      • Request
        • Would you please lower down your voice?

Aspect of Verb

Aspect of Verb

  • Aspects refers to the state of action of a verb

4 Types of Verbs Aspects

  1. Simple Tense
    • Present Tense
      • An uchanging, repeated, or reoccurring action or situation that exist only now. And it can also represents a widespreas truth.
        Example:
        • Unchanging Action
          • The mountain are tall and white.
        • Recurring Action
          • Every year, the school council elects new members.
        • Widespread Truth
          • Pb is the chemical symbol for lead.
    • Past Tense
      • An action or situation that was started and finished in the past.
      • past tense of the verb + "ed".
      • Irregular verbs have special past tense forms which must be memorized.
        Example:
        • Regular -ed past
          • World War II ended in 1945.
        • Irregular form
          • Ernest Hemmingway wrote "The Old Man and the Sea".
    • Future Tense
      • An action or sitiuation that will occur in the future.
      • Will/Shall + simple form of the verb.
        Example:
        • the speaker of the House will finish her term in May of 2020.
  2. Progressive Tense
    • Present Progressive Tense
      • An ongoing action that is happening at the same time the statement is written.
      • Am/Is/Are + verb + "ing".
        Example:
        • The doctor is examining his patient.
    • Past Progressive Tense
      • A past action which was happening when another action occured.
      • Was/Were + verb + "ing"
        Example:
        • The currator was explaining the painting of Juan Luna.
    • Future Progressive Tense
      • An ongoing or continuous action that will take place in the future.
      • Will be/Shall be + verb + "ing"
        Example:
        • Dr. Jones will be presenting ongoing research on sexist language next week.
  3. Perfect Tense
    • Present Prefect Tense
      • An action that happened at an indefinite time in the past or that began in the past and continue in the present.
      • Has/Have + past participle of the verb + "-ed".
      • Irregular verbs have special past participles that must be memorized.
        Examples:
        • The researcher have traveled to many countries in order to collect more significant data.
        • Women have voted in presedential elections since 2019
    • Future Perfect Tense
      • An action that will occur in the future before some other action.
      • had + past particple of the verb.
        Examples:
        • By the time the prof arrives, the students will have spent several hours playing mobile games.
  4. Perfect Progrssive Tense
    • Present Perfect Progressive
      • An action that began in the past, continue in the present, and may continue into the future.
      • Has been/Have been + present participle of the verb + "ing".
        Example:
        • The CEO has been considering a transfer to the state of Texas where profits would be larger.
    • Past Perfect Progressive
      • A past, ongoing action that was completed before some other past action.
      • Had been + present perfect of the verb + "ing".
        Example:
        • Before the budget cuts, the students had been participating in many extracurricular activities.
    • Future Perfect Progressive
      • A future, ongoing action that will occur before some specified future time.
      • Will have been + present participle of the verb + "ing".
        Example:
        • By the year 2022, linguists will have been studying and defining the Indo-European language family for more than 202 years.

Elements of A Short Story

Short Story

  • A story with a fully developed theme but significally shorter and less elaborate than a novel.

5 Elements of a Short Story

  1. Character
    • They are the individuals that takes action in the story.
      • Antagonist - main character
      • Protagonist - opponents
  2. Setting
    • Refers to the time and place of the action.
      • Place - where the action takes place.
      • Time - when is the story taking place.
  3. Conflict
    • Refers to the problem that a character encounters in a story.
      • External - conflict with others and nature.
      • Internal - conflict within one's self.
  4. Theme
    • Refers to the lesson of the story.
  5. Plot
    • It is the sequence of the events in the story.
    • 5 Elements of Plot
      1. Exposition - start of the story.
      2. Rising Action - conflict is revelaed.
      3. Falling Action - reaction to the climax.
      4. Climax - most exciting part/ turning point.
      5. Conclusion - finale
