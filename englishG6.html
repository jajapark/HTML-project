Adjectives

What is Adjective?

  • A word that tells us more about a noun. It "describes" or "modifies" a noun.
    Examples:
    • Adjectives BEFORE a noun:
      • A green car.
    • Adjectives AFTER a verb:
      • The sky became dark

        • Washable/Credible
      • ish/-like
        • Childish/Childlike
      • ful/-less
        • Careful/Careless
      • ous

What is Irregular Adjective?

  • Adjectives that do not chnage form by adding -er/-est or more/most.

Descriptive Words

What is Descriptive Words?

  • Descriptive words are help visualize, describe, define, or explain information about people, places, things, situations, or actions. Most people think of adjectives, words that describe nouns, when they think of descriptive words, but many descriptive words are not adjectives.
    1. Descriptive Words
      • Words that carry clear meanings.
      • Take all of our five senses into account–touch, taste, sight, smell, and sound.
    2. Adjectives
      • Can help describe a person, place, or thing.
    3. Verbs
      • Can also be used carefully to help paint a clearer picture of what someone is doing.
    4. Adverbs
      • Can also be used as descriptive language to help describe action, or how someone does something.

Present Perfect Tense

What is Present Perfect Tense?

  • Comibines the present tense and tge perfect aspects used to express an event that happenned in tha past that has present consequences.
  • Used when talking about experiences from the past, a change or a situation that has happened in the past but is still continuing today.
  • Using present perfect tense...
    • Combine the present tesnse of the verb "to have" + past participle of the main verb of the sentence.
    • The past participle of a regular verb is the base word + "ed".
      • Example:
      • She has lived here all her life.
      • They have written three letters already.
  • Ways on how present prefect tense.
    1. To describe an action that is being repeated between the past and present.
      2. Example:
      • We have gone to the beach many times.
    2. To describe an action that started in the past and is still continuing in the future.
      3. Example:
      • I have lived in the United States since 1990.
    3. To describe an action that has not yet been finished.
    4. Example:
      • It has rained a lot this month.
    5. To describe an action that was completed in the recent past.
      6. Example:
      • I have just finished my internship at the museum.
    6. To describe an action when time was not an important aspect.
      7. Example:
      • She has lost her wedding ring.
  • Ways when you cannot use present perfect tense.
    1. Used with expressions that are unspecific in time.
      2. Example:
      • I have lost my purse.
    2. Used to express an unfinished period of time.
      3. Example:
      • We haven't seen her today.
    3. Uses in talking about events that happened in the recent past but the efecct of the recent event is still felt in the present.
      4. Example:
      • The children have made a mess in the kictchen
    4. Other combination of Words..
      • You can also use time-related adverbs in the present perfect tense, as long as they don't refer to a time which is finished.
      • "already", "just", and "yet"
        • Example:
        • The book came out yesterday, but I have already read it.
      • Can also be used in questions using the words "already" and "yet."
        • Example:
        • Have you called your mom yet?

Lesson 4

Active and Passive Voice

Active and Passive Voice

Active Voice

  • A sentence has a subject that acts upon its verb.
  • When the subject of a sentence performs the verb’s action, we say that the sentence is in the active voice. Sentences in the active voice have a strong, direct, and clear tone.
    • Example:
    • The cashier counted the money

Passive Voice

  • A subject is a recipient of a verb’s action.
  • When the subject is acted on by the verb. It is always constructed with a conjugated form of to be plus the verb’s past participle.
    • Example:
    • The money was counted by the cashier.

Cause and Effect

What is Cause and Effect?

  • Cause and effect are important elements of a text that help the reader to follow a writer’s line of thought, regardless of whether that text is fiction or nonfiction.
  • Cause
    • The driving force in the text and the reason that things happen. In essence, cause is the thing that makes other things happen.
      • Example:
      • Cause: She never brush her teeth.
  • Effect
    • It is what happened next in the text that results from a preceding cause.
      • Example:
      • Effect: She have 5 cavities.

Signal Words and Phrases

  • Signal Words
    • Transitions, are sign posts that help guide the reader through the terrain of the writer’s thoughts.
      • Example:
      • Cause (The Why)
        • Because
        • Because of
        • Since
        • As a result of
        • As a consequence of
        • Now that
      • Effect (The What)
        • So
        • This resulted in
        • Consequently
        • Hence
        • Accordingly
